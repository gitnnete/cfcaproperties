Frequently Asked Questions

In real estate, property refers to land and any structures on it, such as houses, buildings, or natural resources.

The process usually begins with finding a suitable property, then making an offer. If accepted, you proceed to get financing (if needed), complete inspections, and sign contracts. The sale is finalized at closing when you receive the keys and ownership is transferred.

The value of your property can be determined through a professional appraisal, recent sale prices of comparable homes in the area, or an online valuation tool. A real estate agent can also provide a market analysis.

The time it takes to sell a property varies based on location, market conditions, and pricing. On average, it may take 30 to 90 days from listing to closing, but it can be shorter or longer depending on various factors.
